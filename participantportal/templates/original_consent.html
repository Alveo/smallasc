INFORMATION SHEET FOR RESEARCH PARTICIPATION

 

Project Title:            AusTalk: An audio-visual speech corpus of Australian English

 

Support for this study:         Australian Research Council (LIEF Grant LE100100211),

University of Western Sydney, University of Canberra, University of Western Australia, Australian National University, Flinders University, University of Sydney, Macquarie University,

University of Melbourne, University of Tasmania, University of Queensland,

University of New South Wales,

 

 

Invitation to Participate and Description of Project

You are invited to take part in a study to document the speech and the accents of Australians.  The study is a national collaborative study funded by the Australian Research Council and coordinated by MARCS Auditory Laboratories, University of Western Sydney. This study is led by Professor Denis Burnham, Director MARCS Auditory Laboratories and conducted by researchers at each of the 11 institutions participating in the project.

 

The purpose is to create a large audio-visual speech database for Australian English, which will support speech science research and facilitate the development of Australian speech technology applications, for instance telephony-based spoken dialog systems, hearing prostheses, or talking head aids for learning-impaired children.

 

What does the study involve?

If you choose to take part in this study, you will be recorded while reading aloud and talking. The study involves both audio and visual taping.  The sessions are conducted at [Specific location for each Institution] by researchers at [Specific Institution].

 

During each session, you will be asked to read word lists and short written texts. You will also have a casual conversation and you will play a game with another participant.

 

During the recording session, you will wear a headset microphone and the session will be recorded by a camera. Later, the recordings will be stored in a database and analysed by the researchers and their assistants. The recordings will be used in studies about Australian English and the accents of people all around Australia.

 

How much time will the study take?

Each session will take about one hour. You will be expected to make a total commitment of up to 3 hours to the study (in three sessions over a period of 4 to 6 weeks).

 

Will the study involve any discomfort for me?

The study is not intended to involve any discomfort. You will be seated at a table in a comfortable chair. The headset microphone is very light and will be adjusted for you. If you want to discontinue at any point, you are free to withdraw without any penalty.

 

Will the study benefit me?

While there will not be any specific immediate benefits to any individual, the study will benefit all Australians who use any type of speech technology. The study will also contribute to more general research into language and speech, leading to improvements in areas such as education and training.

 

How is this study being paid for?

You will be compensated for your travel expenses by being given $25 for each of the first and second session (whether you decide to complete it or not) and $40 after completion of the third session.

 

Will anyone else know the results? How will the results be disseminated?

The research team will report the results at conferences and in academic journals. All information about the participants will remain confidential. An update about the project and the results will also be published in the local and national media.  If you would like to know about the results, we can send them to you once the study is completed.

 

Excerpts from your recordings may be used also be used for illustrative purposes in teaching, in conference presentations or in various relevant electronic media but you will not be identified if your recordings are used for any of these purposes.

 

Confidentiality

No personal information gathered in this study will be given to any persons other than the researchers and their collaborators unless it is made anonymous – your name will not be used.  Furthermore, no data that you may contribute will be published unless your anonymity is maintained. Information or details regarding the speaker that are gathered in the course of the study are confidential and recordings will be securely stored. In the spirit of scholarly collaboration, speech samples may be made available to other scholars for research purposes.

 

 

Can I withdraw from the study?

Participation is entirely voluntary: you are not obliged to be involved and - if you do participate  - you can withdraw at any time without giving any reason and without any consequences.
